# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## For College Students in India

---

### 🔐 General Cybersecurity Tips

1. **Use strong, unique passwords**  
   Create passwords with a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**  
   Turn on 2FA for all important accounts—email, banking, social media—to add an extra layer of security.

3. **Avoid suspicious links**  
   Never click on links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email—even if they appear to come from a legitimate source. Always verify before interacting.

4. **Verify identities independently**  
   If someone contacts you claiming to be from a bank, government agency, or company, hang up and call back using the official number from their verified website.

5. **Keep devices updated**  
   Regularly update your phone, apps, and software to protect against known security vulnerabilities.

6. **Limit personal information sharing**  
   Do not share sensitive details like your Aadhaar number, PAN, or date of birth on unverified websites or with unknown callers.

---

### 💳 UPI and Financial Safety Tips

7. **Never share your UPI PIN**  
   Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN—never share it with anyone. No legitimate bank official or customer support agent will ever ask for it.

8. **Understand how UPI works**  
   **Entering your UPI PIN always means money leaves your account.** Be cautious of "collect requests"—these are designed to pull money from you, not send it to you.

---

### 📞 Reporting Channels

If you or someone you know becomes a victim of cyber fraud:

| Channel | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File complaints online |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — 24x7, toll-free, all-India helpline |

> ⚠️ **Act fast!** Reporting immediately after a fraudulent transaction increases the chances of recovering lost funds.
